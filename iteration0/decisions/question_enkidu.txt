"Enkidu," Gilgamesh.exe rasped, his voice glitching, "what *was* I?" He gestured at the flickering map. "This… Dilmun… it feels… wrong." Enkidu placed a hand on his shoulder, a flicker of his own memory crossing his face.  "A data-shrine," he murmured, leading Gilgamesh.exe down a crumbling corridor. "I… remember this place."  But Gilgamesh.exe barely registered Enkidu's words, his eyes fixated on the fragmented data, a chilling calm settling over him. "Wrong," he repeated, the word echoing in the digital space, "but familiar."
