"Brother," Gilgamesh echoed, the word a foreign artifact in his fractured memory. The sirens wailed closer.  "We need to move." Enkidu nodded, melting into the shadows.  They found refuge in a derelict pachinko parlor, the air thick with the scent of stale cigarettes and desperation. A holographic woman, Siduri, materialized from a flickering console.  "Gilgamesh," she purred, "I've been expecting you. I know of Dilmun."  Enkidu scoffed, arms crossed. "Don't trust her," he hissed.  Siduri smiled, unfazed. "Dilmun," she repeated, "a gateway to transcendence."
